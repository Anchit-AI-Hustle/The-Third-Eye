The problem

Creators are running a business on duct tape

A working creator juggles six disconnected tools — and brands still can't tell who's real.

🧩

Fragmented tooling

Scheduling in one app, link-in-bio in another, affiliate codes in a spreadsheet, a website nowhere. Nothing talks to each other.

🕵️

No trust layer

Brands can't easily verify a creator's identity or that their audience is genuine, so deals stall and payouts are slow.

💸

Monetisation gap

Only ~8–10% of Indian creators monetise, vs 40%+ in mature markets — mostly because they lack the commerce rails.

Source: BCG analysis, cited by Kotak MF, 2025
🏢

Brands fly blind

Discovery is manual and relationship-based; there's no verified, structured place to find and transact with the right creators.

  • Turn affiliate codes into a shoppable storefront followers can buy from
  • Spin up a marketing website with SEO + Google / Meta / TikTok / print ad connectors
  • Aadhaar-verified identity — the trust layer brands pay for
  • A two-sided marketplace: brands discover verified creators and run commission deals

Business model

Three revenue lines

🎯

Creator subscriptions

Basic & Pro monthly/annual plans. Predictable, recurring SaaS revenue.

🏢

Brand subscriptions

Brands pay for marketplace access to verified creators + campaign tooling.

💰

Commission take-rate

A small % on marketplace deals and affiliate GMV flowing through Kolab.

Illustrative unit economics — to validate: Pro creator at ₹800–999/mo → ~₹10–12K ARPU/yr. Blended CAC target < 1/3 of first-year value. Marketplace take-rate compounds as GMV grows.

Go-to-market

Seed the supply, then pull demand

  • Phase 1 — Creators: onboard a founding cohort (fitness, lifestyle, finance niches) with complimentary access; make them power users and case studies.
  • Phase 2 — Storefront virality: every creator storefront is a public, shareable page that markets Kolab to their audience for free.
  • Phase 3 — Brands: with verified supply in place, sell marketplace subscriptions to brands and agencies.
  • Channels: creator referrals, niche communities, and partnerships with agencies & MCNs.
